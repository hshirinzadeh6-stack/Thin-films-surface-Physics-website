Introduction to basic Vacuum technology,
this course introduces fundamental concepts of vacuum technology through use of the kinetic theory of gasses. Mathematical expression are presented, explained conceptually and related to the things we actually measure (pressure, temperature, composition). Flow, conductance and vacuum system design are introduced. Different types of pumps and gauges are examined in terms of how they operate and their advantages and disadvantages. An introduction to residual gas analysis in includes.

  Introduction to thin film technology
This full day introduction to the broad field of thin film technology begins with an overview of the film growth process in general terms. we examine basic concepts of crystal structure and defects in crystalline thin film such as grain boundaries, dislocations and vacancies, we then consider the general nature of thin film growth from atoms equilibrating with the service, though the initial stages of growth to film coalescence and zone models. The remainder of the class examines evaporation.sputer deposition and chemical vapor deposition in detail to show how the principles discussed are applied in real systems.

Topics:
What is thin film?
Science background
  1. Overview of thin film growth
  2. Crystal structure and defects (dislocations, grain boundaries)
  3. Diffusion
  4. Properties of vacuum
Film formation
  • Thermal accommodation
  • Sticking and surface diffusion
  • Nucleation of film
  • Growth modes (island, layer by layer, mixed)
  • Coalescence growth (zone models)
Other factors
  • Energetic deposition
  • Amorphous films
  • Epitaxial growths
Deposition parameters and techniques
  • Evaporation
  • Vacuum Arc deposition
Sputtering deposition
  • DC
  • Rf
Chemical vapor deposition
Ion beam assisted deposition
Plasma enhance deposition (PECVD, ECR…)
Spin coating

Thin film Characterization Basics
These courses introduce the basic concepts of the characyeration of thin film materials. We will examine methods for characterization the physical structure, chemical composition, optical properties, electrical properties, magnetic properties, and mechanical properties (stress, hardness, adhesion) of thin film materials, due to time limitations, divice characterization will not be included. The course is suitable for engineers and techniques, as well as to application engineers and technical marking people needing an introduction to this important subject


Topics:
  • Overview of thin film characterization
  • Introduction to thin film characterization techniques
  • Images:
  • Optical microscopy
  • Scanning electron microscopy (SEM)
  • Transmission electron microscopes (STM)
  • Scanning probe micros copies (STM, AFM…)
  • Structural properties:

  • Stylus profilometry
  • Quartz crystal monitors (QCM)

  • Chemical /structural properties
  • Auger electron spectroscopy (AES)
  • Energy Dispersive Analysis of X-rays (EDAX)

  • Rutherford Backscattering (RBS)

  • Optical / Structural properties

  • Magnetic properties
